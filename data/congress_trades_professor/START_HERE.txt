QUICK START (3 STEPS)
================================================================================

Step 1: Find your congressional trade ticker
  Example: Rep. X bought SPLK stock on Feb 15, 2024

Step 2: Look up in TICKER_MAPPING_WITH_DAILY_DATA.csv
  → Find "SPLK" in ticker_symbol column
  → See updated_ticker_for_data = "CSCO" (acquired by Cisco)
  → Check status = "ACQUIRED"
  → Check data available from 2010-2026

Step 3: Query Snowflake for the UPDATED ticker
  → SELECT date, open, high, low, close, volume
  → FROM JARVIS.LSEG_DAILY
  → WHERE ticker = 'CSCO'
  → AND date = '2024-02-15'
  → Use CSCO price as your data point

That's it! You now have price data for the delisted ticker.

================================================================================
COMMON ISSUES & SOLUTIONS
================================================================================

Q: Can't find a ticker in the mapping?
A: Check spelling, try delisted_tickers_research.csv, or it might be in the
   ~53 unreachable ones (international/private equity/small-cap).

Q: Updated_ticker_for_data is blank or same as original?
A: That's normal - the company still trades, use it directly in Snowflake.

Q: What if the congressional trade date is after the acquisition date?
A: The stock would have stopped trading under the original ticker. Use the
   acquiring company's ticker, or it might be data entry error in the original.

Q: Multiple rows for the same ticker?
A: Normal - companies trade on multiple exchanges. Pick the one with most
   data_points (usually US primary exchange).

See README_CONGRESSIONAL_TRADING_DATASET.md for full troubleshooting section.

================================================================================
SNOWFLAKE QUERY EXAMPLES
================================================================================

Get daily data for a ticker:
  SELECT date, open, high, low, close, volume
  FROM JARVIS.LSEG_DAILY
  WHERE ticker = 'CSCO'
  AND date BETWEEN '2024-02-01' AND '2024-03-31'

Get returns:
  SELECT date, close,
         ROUND((close - LAG(close) OVER (ORDER BY date)) /
               LAG(close) OVER (ORDER BY date) * 100, 2) as daily_return_pct
  FROM JARVIS.LSEG_DAILY
  WHERE ticker = 'CSCO'

Get summary stats:
  SELECT MIN(close) as min_price, MAX(close) as max_price,
         AVG(close) as avg_price, COUNT(*) as trading_days
  FROM JARVIS.LSEG_DAILY
  WHERE ticker = 'CSCO'
  AND date BETWEEN '2024-02-01' AND '2024-03-31'

See README for more examples.
